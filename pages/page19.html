Definitions
Structural steel

Typically includes all main frame building, stair towers, bridge crane supports and pipe racks. Formwork: is the term given to either temporary or permanent moulds into which concrete or similar materials are poured.

Stability of Structures
Contractor ensure that during any construction activities (erection or dismantling operations of new or existing structures do not result in a temporary state of weakness, instability or collapse of the side structure, any formwork/false-work or temporary structure shall be designed by a competent structural engineer who meets RC and SCE Requirement, no part of any structure shall be overloaded as render it unsafe.
Structure Steelwork
Contractor shall conduct a RA for all erection and dismantling of structural steelwork, contractor shall submit a safe sequence of works in accordance with Standard 204, the safe sequence of work must address the following but not limited to :(specific logical sequence of erection, method of erection, preparation of bases, shim packs or leveling nuts, constructability issues, alignment checks, allowable specified tolerances, bolting-up/welding of connections, torqueing methodology, involvement of supplier representative, lifting and rigging plan, equipment to be used for working at height, fall protection, labor tools and equipment requirements, delivery and unloading methods, permits, worker training, other safety requirement, final inspection and commissioning), no erection or dismantling of structural steelwork is to commence before the safe sequence of works has been approved by RC, structural steel erection cannot commence until the concrete in the footings/piers and walls and mortar in the masonry piers and walls, construction activities associated with the erection of structural steel must conform the above references.
Formwork/ False-work
All formwork and false-work shall be designed by a structure engineer and drawings, contractor is to ensure that the base the formworks or false-works sits upon is adequate to support the weight of the formwork and concrete and any additional live loads, all formwork and false-work materials support frames/jacks/U-shape/bearers are to comply with relevant specs, erection and dismantling of formwork and falsework shall be under the supervision of a competent civil engineer, partially erected or dismantled formwork or false-work, once erected formwork and false-work shall be inspected and certified as ready-for-use, scaffolding or lightweight staging must not be used for falsework, props for either falseworks or formwork must be secured in place to prevent accident, usually where formwork falseworks have been used in the construction activity, all working at height must comply with standards 214, only the civil engineer in the charge of the particular works may give instruction for the striking for each section of formwork, provide emergency plan.
