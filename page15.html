STD-221 Lifting Operations (Personnel & Planning)


Definition
Lifting operation Equipment

Cranes and all associated lifting tackle equipment such as spreader bars, lifting frames, spacers, slings, strops, chains and shackles.

Standard
Lifting Personnel

Appointed Competent Person (lifting & Rigging) shall: (competent civil/mechanical/structural engineer, 3ed party training, qualified for rigger classifications, suitable experience approved by RC, access and plan all lifts, devise and compile all lift calculation sheets, be in overall control of all lifting operation, ensure that an effective procedure is in space for inspection and maintenance of all lifting operation, subsequent remedial actions, be present on site for all lifts over 10 tones, be authorized to cancel/postpone lifts).

Standard
Crane operators shall

(Hold a valid SA heavy equipment operator's license, be trained to operate the specific crane, be competent and authorized to operate, be physically fit and capable, be authorized to cancel postpone lifts if there is safety concerns).

Standard
Banksman/Slingers/Riggers shall

( be trained/experience and hold a current approved certificate, be able to determine the weight, be able to inspect and determine whether associated lifting equipment is damaged, be familiar with different and correct slinging techniques, know correct hand signals, be authorized to cancel, complete a "Rigger's Daily Checklist").

Standard
Lift Planning

Any lift where the payload weight is 10 tones or less requires only a lift calculation sheet and contractor appointed competent person and crane operator prior to work commencing, any lift where the payload weight is over 10-50 tones requires industry-standard lift plan to be submitted to and received by RC for approval a min of 2 working day, for all lifts that are critical, lift plans must be submitted to and received by RC, Critical lifts are defined as (all lifts where the payload is over 50 tones, any tandem 2 or more cranes lifts, any lift regardless of payload weight, any lift over live feedstock pipelines, barge cranes).
