Work at Height

Above or below ground.

Scaffold erector

Hold 3ed party 'Scaffold erection' training certificate.

Scaffold Competent Person

responsible for all scaffolds on site.

Open tube & Clamp Scaffold

A versatile type of scaffold consisting of steel tubes and clamps.

System Scaffold

Different sized vertical posts and horizontal and diagonal tubes, fixed connection points spaced regular intervals and horizontal and diagonal tubes

General Standards

Working at height where RA show that scaffolding is to be constructed only open tube & clamp or system scaffold is acceptable by RC. Mixing different types of scaffolding shall not be employed note that H-frame is prohibited. Planning include RA and safe sequence of work include in ES&H. scaffold over 10m long and 8m in height must be designed by a qualified/professional structure engineer, no alternations or deviations from the original design is allowed, 3ed party can erect/modify/maintain or dismantle scaffolding, constructed of sound strong material, tube should not be split/cracked/rusty/bent/distorted and ends should be cut square and clean, each scaffold and scaffold component support without failure its own weight and at least four time the man weight and they must not be overloaded above rated capacities, adequate and suitable tools and suitable training for erection/modification/moving/maintaining/inspection/dismantling. During erection/modification/moving/dismantling of scaffolding only competent must have fitted scaffold inspection tagging system, scaffold should be locked-off and any access point at the end of each day.

Foundations

Adequate strength for support and disperse the load of the scaffold, sufficient strength and thickness, standards may be placed directly onto the surface, scaffold boards that have been used as sole boards must not be used on the main scaffold, for system scaffolds bay lengths are set at 2.4m.

Working Platform

Adequate with to suit the work being carried out, working platforms, gangways and access points are to be kept free from obstructions.

Ties

Ties: scaffolds prevented from collapsing or tipping by secure attachment to the bearing surface or to another structure, Reveal tie(wedged or jacked tight into opposing faces for an opening), through tie()
