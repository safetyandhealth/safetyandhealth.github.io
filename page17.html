Definitions

Attendant

an individual stationed outside the confined space who monitors the entrants and controls and monitors entry and exit of the confined space.

Authorized entrant

An employee who is trained & authorized to enter a confined space.

Hazardous atmosphere

expose employee to the risk of death incapacitation, impairment of ability to self-rescue, injury or acute or chronic illness.

Confined Space

(pipe, duct, tank, trench, pit, recess , sewer, vat, silo, large enough that an employee can enter and perform work, may contain hazardous materials, be oxygen-deficient, have a flammable atmosphere, limited or restricted means for entry and exit, designed such that entrapment or crushing could occur, not designed for continuous occupancy).

Entry Supervisor

competent person responsible for determining if acceptable conditions are present in a confined space where entry is planned for authorizing and overseeing entry and for terminating entry.

Standards

Contractor must appoint a competent person to supervise entry into confined spaces, submit to RC for review and approval, submit plan containing risk assessment to RC for approval in accordance with contract special condition 20

Training

Training for confined space entry training mandatory for all personnel who will control entry, records of training are to be kept for the entire contract on site, training delivered to all persons must consist of the following topics: (functions of the attendant, means of communicating, entry and explosive atmosphere hazards, signs/symptoms/consequences of exposure, testing and monitoring equipment to be used, correct use of communications, evacuation signals and alarms, signs and symptoms of heat stress injuries, behavioral effects of hazard exposure of the entrants, how to communicate with entrants, how to alert entrants of the need to evacuate the confined space, how and when to order an evacuation, how to prevent unauthorized access to the confined space, how and when to perform non-entry rescue, test/procedures and equipment, how and when to endorse the permit to allow entry, when to terminate the entry and cancel the permit, how t determine that acceptable conditions).

Equipment requirements

The following must be made available by the contractor (Ventilating equipment, communication system, PPE, Temporary lighting, hand & power tools, signage, barriers, non-entry rescue equipment, other equipment)

Special precautions

Depending upon the scope of work/location and results of the risk assessment the following: ( co-ordination with other employers, co-ordination with property owners, co-ordination with service provided, lock-out/tag-out procedures of energy, isolation of processes and process lines, hot work required) .

Pre-entry

Contractor ES&H manager and entry supervisor shall visit the confined space and review their risk assessment and safe sequence of work to the actual conditions, sequence of work shall be reviewed whenever there is a reason to believe that measures taken may not be protecting the employees, pre-tasking briefing must then be conducted, TABLE for ACTION and RESPOSIBILITIES.

Entry Testing and Monitoring

The entry supervisor and contractor ES&H Manager must then perform pre-entry testing to determine if acceptable entry conditions are present prior to entry, if it is necessary to enter a hazardous atmosphere to perform testing, a positive pressure, pressure demand self-contained breathing apparatus, the entry supervisor and Contractor ES&H shall test the internal atmosphere of the confined space before any employee enters.

Permit to work (Confined Space) System

A confined space entry permit must be completed prior to any work, Supervisor and contractor ES&H Manager are the only employees authorized to sign the permit and authorize entry, completed and signed permit posted at the entry of the confined space prior to the time of entry so that entrants can confirm control measures are I place, duration of the permit must not exceed the time required to complete the task, the entry supervisor shall terminate entry to a confined space and cancel the permit when:(authorized time of the permit has expired, works has been completed, a condition not allowed under the permit arises in or near the confined space, an emergency or incident requiring evacuation of the area), attendant must monitor the internal atmosphere of the confined space at a min, in event a hazardous atmosphere is detected after entry operation have begun :( evacuate, account for all entrants, prevent re-entering) by attendant, ( evaluate and determine how hazard happened, implement engineering control to reduce the hazardous, document) by supervisor, Manager inform the RC ES&H Dep.

Emergencies & Emergency Services

ES&H Manager shall co-ordinate with off-site rescue services before the start on any works on the project to determine (availability and extent of services, back-up arrangement, response, emergency phone numbers, availability of standby, any special requirement), ES&H Manager shall provide the off-site rescue services(, contractor medical staff shall have copies of all MSDS, where feasible entry rescues will only be performed by qualified off-site emergency personnel, only employees fully trained in attendant duties shall perform non-entry rescues.
