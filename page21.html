STD-301 Heat & Cold Stress Injuries


Definition

Heat injuries

heat stress disorders, heat exhaustion or heat hyperpyrexia (stroke).

Cold injuries

Frostbite, hypothermia or immersion foot.

Heat Index

is an index that combines air temperature and relative humidity in an attempt to determine the human-perceived equivalent temperature.

General Requirements

Contractor's ES&H program shall contain a dedicated procedure for reducing exposure to heat and cold injuries for work conditions in SA, Ministerial Decree 3337- prohibited to work under direct sun in open areas form 12 p.m. to 3p.m. during the period from the 15th day of June to 15th of Sept.

The following factors shall be considered: (mean temperatures, wind speeds, PPE and clothing that must be worn, type of work, nationality of the workforce heath fo workforce, level of heat and cold injury awareness of workforce), any employee who has symptoms of any heat or cold injury shall seek immediate medical attention, contractor is the provider awareness training on symptoms and first aid treatment for heat injuries on monthly form April-October, contractor to comply with Standard 302 welfare specifically provision of shade and cooled portable water, it is not envisaged that employees will be exposed to cold injuries due to the environment in SA however processes do contain the hazards of injuries.

Contractor project nurse will visit the site with complete PPE and medical uniform on daily basis and on time of leave or absence a replacement must be available, project nurse must conduct/participate in health related training according to the type of work and conditions of the workers, heat index reach 54 or greater task must be stopped immediately, heat index varies from 39-53 contractor must follow extreme cautions according to heat stress prevention plan.
