Definitions

Qualified person

trained, knowledgeable, experienced in the construction and operation of equipment or a specific work method and be qualified to recognize and avoid the electrical hazards that might be present with respect or work method.

Voltage

difference of potential between any two conductors in a circuit or between a conductor and ground. Current: the flow of electrical energy.

Resistance Impedance

the characteristic of material or a machine that resists flow of current and in coordination with voltage an current allows the machine to create mechanical or thermal energy.

Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI):

an electrical device for personal protection that disconnects a circuit. Grounding: intentionally or unintentionally connection any object to the ground directly or indirectly.

Standard

(the standards applies to all electrical cord sets and receptacles not part of the permanent electrical wiring system of a building or structure. Only qualified electricians may set-up, modify, inspect and repair electrical circuits, lock-out/tag-out and permit to work system must be in place, all live electrical panels must be physically lock-out, lock-out/tag-out will be issued controlled and closed by the senior electrician, all electricians are to be trained, permits to work must be kept for the entire length of the project or revised, unqualified unauthorized persons shall not open any electrical enclosure or apparatus, once the building or structure is energized no exposed wires are permitted on live circuits, employees will visually inspect receptacles, extension cables cards and equipment connected cord and plug before each day’s sue in order to determine if there are external defects, no electrical tools must be used without a plug and no extension cables may be used with damaged outlets, in the event a coworker becomes overcome be an electrical circuit a nearby person should not attempt to grab and pull him t safety, all site offices shall be equipped with SASO 2203 compliant sockets, all fluorescent light fittings used on site or in the office area shall be provided with a protective cover.

Assured Equipment Grounding Program

An electrical continuity and polarity test for all circuitry shall be performed by a qualified person on all cords (before first use of the equipment, at intervals not to exceed 3 months, before repaired equipment is returned to service, before equipment is used after any incident that may have caused damage), all electrical equipment that is to be plugged into these cords, all tests will be make recorded and retained by the senior electrician on site, all equipment that undergo this test will be marked with electrician’s tapes and will conform to the color code, any equipment not passing the continuity test will be taken out of service, all repaired equipment must pass the continuity test before returning to service

GFCI and Assured equipment grounding program

Applicable codes and standards are not more stringent, GFCI’s will be provided for all 120V and 110V, single phase, 15 20 30 amp receptacle outlets, where applicable GFCI may be required according to national standards, where reasonable GFCI’s must be located as near as possible to the equipment being used, GFCI’s are to be inspected on a quarterly basis and color coded as serial 15 above.

Generator

All generator and transformers will be maintained in a good condition, generator shall have sufficient capacity for the application for which they used, any rotating components will be guarded, the area around generators shall be maintained free of fuel and oil spills, external fuel tanks will be located in areas with containment provisions, all generator shall be directed to ground system, outlets will be in good condition with no exposed conductors, generators are to be shaded and protected from the elements silent type enclosed generator should be provided, adequate hazard warning signage is to be displayed no smoking and hearing protection, generator locations are to be supplied with relevant fire extinguisher and hearing protection

Distribution (Temporary)

Distribution panels shall carry an international “electrical Hazard” warning sign consisting of a yellow triangle with a black lightning, panels dept. locked and key with senior electrician, boards have an RCD/GFCI fitted on the circuit breaker of all circuits that feed a wet area or a circuit which feeds power tools, boards shall be weather-proofed and grounded and have a unique number, boards shall be provided with a ground terminal strip for the termination of all ground wires, terminal points will be in an enclosure with feed and outlet cables channeled through the enclosure via rubber/plastic grommets to prevent insect infestation, cables to and from distribution boards shall be clearly identified and labeled the point at which the distribution board, temporary electrical power shall not be tapped off from any part of a permanent electrical wire system,

all cables shall have a unique number and shall be a size and rating suitable, splicing of cables is not permitted, domestic standard cables and fittings shall not be allowed on site, particular attention shall be given to cable management to ensure that cables are routed in such a manner as to prevent an obstruction/ cable damage, precautions shall be taken to ensure that cables cannot come into direct contact with metal scaffolding, all cables on site must be terminated at both ends, all circuit breakers shall be selected based on the loads to which they are connected, load & voltage drop checks shall be performed on the site systems by senior electrician, the senior electrician must maintain an up to date single line diagram of the site electrical system in his office. .

Electrical Hand Tools

The only electrical hand tools that can be used on site are either 120/110V mains operated or battery operated, equipment casings will be intact with no loose fittings or exposed cables, plug & socket fittings shall be of an approved industrial type, tools shall be subjective to preventative maintenance schedules as per manufacturer’s recommendations, contractors must conduct scheduled inspections of all electrical hand tools.

Overhead Transmission / Power lines

Where overhead power transmission line exist on or adjacent to construction sites or access roads to the site, plan the methods to be used to prevent contact with the overhead lines by the following activities:( lifting operation, tipping trucks, construction plant, man-lifts, scaffolding, ladders). As a general rule an SEC encroachment permit must be obtained where works requires construction plant, RA must be conducted by the contractor to identify control measures to be used such as goal posts. !
