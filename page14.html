Definition

Formwork

The term given to either temporary or permanent moulds into which concrete or similar material are poured.

Consists of temporary structures used in construction to support spanning or arched structures in order to hold the component in place until its construction is sufficiently advanced to support itself. False-work also includes temporary support structures for formwork used to mold concrete to form a desired shape.

Lifting Operation Equipment

Cranes and all other associated lifting tackle equipment such as spreader bars, lifting frames, spacers, slings, strops, chains and shackles.

Standard

RC construction Department require a Method Statement for all concrete placement( Logical sequence of operations and emergency response plan, Details of Formwork, Details of Reinforcing steel and Rebar, Tying details for rebar structures, Access & egress to the placement site, segregated pedestrian walkways, resources required including construction plat, training of the workforce, fall protection required, night working lighting 50 lux, securing access to the concrete placement site, safety induction of concrete equipment operators and equipment inspection,

Traffic Management Plan, Conducting pre pour meeting which should include the quantity of pour). RA and Safe Sequence of Works for all concreting operations to be in Construction Phase ES&H Plan in accordance with standard 204 (PPE worn, control of noise, Control of vibration form concreting vibratory equipment, Weather conditions).

Unloading of Vehicle

On delivery to site/bundles of rebar must not be unloaded by hand / no dragging off/ reinforcing materials are to be off loaded by either a crane.

Formwork/ False-work

All formwork and false-work shall be designed and built in accordance with Standard 225, embedded items and anchor bolt shall be correctly supported, all ends of reinforcing bars must be protected to prevent impalement; plastic caps are not sufficient.

Access & Working Platform

Usually where formwork is constructed there is a requirement for a working platform, working platform requirements and access are to be in accordance with 216 Working Platforms.

Construction Plant and Equipment

All construction plat shall be fit for purpose operated and maintained in a safe manner by a competent person, concrete pumps with placing booms shall be sited correctly with min 2m away from excavation edges and outriggers fully extended, ground bearing pressure calculations shall include as part of the Method Statement with expected dynamic loading stresses taken into consideration, all moving parts of concrete pumps concrete mixers and other associated concreting equipment shall be guarded specifically rotating parts, plastic sheeting must be place underneath concrete pump/concrete truck to avoid possible ground contamination, the discharging end of the placement boom must be under control concrete placement, fixed concrete saws shall only be operated by competent persons with proof of training, concrete skips/buckets shall have dedicated lifting equipment party inspected on an annual basis capable and tested to withstanding 3 times the maximum load of the skip, suitable control measures shall be out in place when concrete cutting takes place to mitigate the risk posed by silica dust, all lifting equipment shall conform to Standard 222 lifting equipment, during operation the rider shall wear a full body harness with lifeline attached independently to main crane hook or headache ball.

Training

Contractor shall ensure the workforce is trained on all equipment and tools used during the concrete operation.

Personal Protective Equipment

MSDS control measures for concrete/ sealants/ form release agents/ curing compounds and all other chemicals involved during concrete operations that are to be used, contractor shall ensure that all MSDS personal Protective Equipment, the min requirements for PPE for all concreting operation (safety helmet, safety glasses, non-permeable, safety boots, rubber overalls for concrete placement boom operator, hearing protection, face-shield for persons holding concrete placement hose).
