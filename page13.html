Definition

Excavation:

A cavity formed by cutting, digging or scooping the earth by manual or mechanical means. Trench: A long, usually narrow and steep-sided excavation less than 15 feet wide.

Standard

Contractor must appoint a competent person to supervise excavations/trenching/backfilling, commission and develop a geotechnical report(including hazardous or potentially contaminated material) to determine the characteristics and properties of the soil material and provide geotechnical conclusions and recommendations for the design of control measures for the project, geotechnical report should provide valuable information for use in determining (Dewatering requirements, Slope stability, Soil classification, ground support requirement, shoring requirements, ground hardness, suitability of materials for excavation, guidelines for equipment selection, ground pressure guidelines by depth, estimated swelling adjusting factor, location of aquifers, soil contaminants, other related issues to support site earthwork activities), may be necessary to perform additional borings or subsurface investigations to identify subsurface utilities, submit the RA and Safe sequence of Work, the prequalification and selection of material testing laboratories, non-potable water and borrow-pit sources for earthworks activities

Dewatering operation

If excavation is expected to encounter water through groundwater/leaking pipes/storm water then a dewater system must be provided by contractor.

Permit to Dig

Prior to initiating excavation or trenching operations, competent person to verify encroachment permits in place and work site conditions including location and detection of all utilities and services, competent person issue a Utility Clearance form (excavation permit to dig), ensure the latest revision of as-laid(as-build) drawings have been used to locate all underground installations, the excavation competent person shall note on the Utility Clearance from any special requirements and control measures required for excavation.

Inspection

Daily inspection of excavations and trench shall be performed by competent, JHA to be completed each work shift before worker access any excavation.

General

Offices

Detection of Buried Services must be adhered to all excavation and trenching operations, excavators and power tools must not be within 0.5m of the indicated line of services only hand digging is permitted, cut at the angle of repose for the soil conditions, all spoil material excavated must be kept a distance away from the edge equal to depth of the excavation, A means of access and egress shall be provided every 7.6m for all excavation and trenches, signs/warnings and barricades for all excavations and trenches and excavations must have a rigid hard barricade at all time, soft barricades must be a min of 1.8m away from the open edges of the excavation or trench, if there is a risk of collapse of the excavation wall due to surcharge then supports system are to be designed by a registered civil engineer, whilst in an excavation or trench, no employees are permitted within 10m of construction plat working/vehicle /lifting operation, tipping materials into an excavation or trench well anchored stop-blocks must be used at a min distance of 1.8m form the edge to prevent vehicle overrunning the edge, trained flagmen/bank men (Redman) are permitted to go near any construction plant or vehicle, consider erosion control measures when using dewatering operations, if an unexpected item of archeological interest is encounter, work must be stopped and RC informed, worker must be trained.

Excavations

Deeper than 1.2 the contractor must use a recognized protective system: (Shoring, Sloping, Benching) and they are designed by registered professional engineer and manufacturer’s instructions, excavations 6.08m access must be restricted and means of monitoring access and egress at the entrance must be established.

Trenches

1.2m or more must be timber-shored, all trench ground support systems may be designed by a qualified structural engineer and installed by competent persons and manufacturer’s instructions followed.

Backfill Operations

The start of backfilling, the excavation competent person shall confirm that all utilities have been inspected tested as required and adequately protected, excavation shall be cleaned of all waste and slurries and all other unacceptable materials.

Specialist Operations

Contractor must appoint a competent sub-contractor for all tunnel Boring machine (TBM) or Horizontal Directional Drilling (HDD) Operations and sub-contractor must be approved by RC, RA and Safe Sequence of Work of TBM or HDD operations specific to the contract and be approved by RC.
